Think of the BIP-39 English wordlist as a special secret-codebook that crypto wallets use to turn a long string of random numbers into a short, easy-to-remember phrase made of everyday words—like “apple”, “river”, or “planet”. A wallet picks a few of those words (usually 12 or 24) and writes them down; anyone who knows the exact words can rebuild the wallet and its money. The list has exactly 2,048 words chosen so they’re simple to spell and unlikely to be confused with each other, making the backup both safe and user-friendly. BIP-39 English word list (GitHub).
